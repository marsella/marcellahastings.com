I'm a computer science and math major with a studio art minor at Tufts University School of Engineering, class of 2015.

I like watching people talk about things that they love to do. I like being surrounded by people who are motivated to work on their thing, whatever it might be. I like enabling those around me to plan the events, share the knowledge, and build the projects they've been considering.

Luckily, encouraging any one of these things has a net benefit on the others.

When I'm not talking about computer science, I enjoy organizing, arts and crafts projects, and cooking dinner.

Interested in collaborating, promoting a project, or getting coffee? Send me a note, let's make it happen.

Selected Projects

Computer Science Exchange


The Computer Science Exchange is the undergraduate computer science group at Tufts University. Our mission is to promote community in the Computer Science department by providing workshops, social opportunities, field trips, and support to students. I've served as president since January 2012.

ColorMe


ColorMe is a song visualizer that lets you enter your favorite artist and see a representation of their most popular songs in color. Made at the Echo Nest Music Hack Day in November 2013.

Tic Tac Toe


Won third place with the classic tic-tac-toe game implemented using Elm, a functional reactive language. Created at the Fall 2013 Tufts Hackathon, as an exercise in using Haskell-like languages to do cool things.
To play the game, use the Elm Emulator and paste in the source code.

Tensors


Semester project for an Advanced Functional Programming course that procedurally generates a street map based on a set of user-provided constraints. The idea is based on a paper by Peter Wonka, and was developed in Haskell, including such improvements as a simplified input/output scheme and highly modularized components. The fully functional code and our final paper are available on Github.
